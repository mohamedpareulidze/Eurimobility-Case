PESTEL analysis – external forces shaping Eurimobility’s sourcing

Political, economic and regulatory pressure all converge toward the same direction: reducing Asian dependency, increasing European sourcing and building a more resilient, compliant battery and electronics supply chain.

P
Political
Sovereignty & geopolitics
Explore
  • Geopolitical tensions and shifting alliances.
  • EU rules lowering CO₂ emissions and pushing clean industry.
  • Trade tensions with China and export controls.
  • Instability of key logistics corridors (Suez Canal, Red Sea, straits).
  • Critical Raw Materials Act (CRMA) encouraging EU sourcing and processing.
E
Economic
Cost & market volatility
Explore
  • Volatile Lithium & Copper prices (~+20% vs base).
  • High freight and logistics costs after the post-COVID shock.
  • Dependence on low-cost labour in Asia (e.g. Vietnam).
  • Inflation on electronic components (chips, PCB).
S
Social
Expectations & perception
Explore
  • CSR importance in tenders and supplier selection.
  • Demand for sustainable mobility and low-carbon products.
  • Preference for European-made goods from customers and public opinion.
  • Growing demand for social transparency in the supply chain.
T
Technological
Tools & innovation
Explore
  • Rise of digital procurement tools and advanced analytics.
  • NLP for supplier intelligence and text-mining on regulations and news.
  • Jaggaer e-sourcing & auctions as standard procurement platforms.
  • Dependence on Asian semiconductors and power electronics.
  • Smart sensors in mobility devices increasing electronics content.
E
Environmental
CO₂ & sustainability
Explore
  • New EU battery regulation on footprint and recyclability.
  • CO₂-heavy imports vs nearshoring and local-for-local strategies.
  • Pressure from customers and regulators for greener supply chains.
  • Carbon pricing on long-distance imports increases cost of Asian flows.
L
Legal
Compliance & duty of care
Explore
  • Mandatory metal traceability and due-diligence obligations.
  • EU taxonomy and green-finance requirements.
  • Duty of Vigilance law on human rights and environmental risks.
  • Anti-greenwashing regulations making transparency mandatory.
