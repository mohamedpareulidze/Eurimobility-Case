SWOT analysis – procurement position of Eurimobility

A consolidated view of strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats guiding the 2026 procurement roadmap and the shift towards more European, resilient sourcing.

Strengths

  • Strong European industrial expertise.
  • Advanced tools: SAP, Jaggaer e-sourcing, BI dashboards.
  • Good reputation for quality and innovation.
  • Clear supplier scorecard (QCDP).
  • Long-term supplier relationships strengthening resilience.

Opportunities

  • EU funding for reshoring and green sourcing.
  • AI, automation and reverse auctions to improve efficiency.
  • European suppliers becoming more competitive.
  • ESG-driven tenders favour local, responsible supply chains.
  • EU Battery Regulation & CRMA accelerating European sourcing.

Strengths Weaknesses Opportunities Threats

Weaknesses

  • 63% of spend with Asian suppliers (high dependency).
  • 28% of categories with a single supplier.
  • ESG and compliance processes still immature.
  • Few strategic partnerships with European suppliers.
  • Purchasing too cost-driven, limited risk & resilience modeling.

Threats

  • China–EU trade tensions and geopolitical shocks.
  • Disruptions: semiconductor shortage, shipping crises.
  • Rising prices of lithium and copper.
  • High switching costs when changing key suppliers.
  • Reputation and legal risk on ethics, traceability and CO₂ rules.
