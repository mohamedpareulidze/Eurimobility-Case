Stakeholder expectations & areas of resistance

Managing acceptability of the 2026 sourcing strategy

The shift toward increased European sourcing impacts several stakeholder groups. Understanding expectations and areas of resistance is key to securing buy-in and successfully implementing the roadmap.

Stakeholder matrix – expectations vs. resistance
Stakeholder Expectations Areas of resistance
Clients Quality, traceability, regulatory compliance, competitive pricing. Fear of short-term cost increases.
Shareholders Profitability, reduced geopolitical risks, strong CSR reputation. Concerns about CAPEX required for relocation.
Employees Job security, skills development, stability of European sites. Resistance to changes in processes and tools.
Suppliers Continuity of volumes, clear contractual conditions, support during transition. Historical Asian suppliers reluctant to lose volumes → risk of commercial tensions.
