How the sourcing strategy is applied by category

The reduction of Asian dependency is mainly driven by three strategic families. Each one has a clear sourcing playbook combining dual sourcing, new European partners and structured risk mitigation.

Electronic Components
Strategic · High risk
  • Shift from China-heavy sourcing to EU/Asia dual sourcing.
  • Qualify new European PCB & power electronics suppliers.
  • Secure long-term contracts to stabilise capacity and prices.
Li-ion Batteries
Strategic · High impact
  • Build partnerships with ACC, Northvolt, Verkor, and other EU gigafactories.
  • Gradually shift volumes from Asian suppliers to European plants.
  • Use CO₂, recycling content and traceability as key selection criteria.
Wiring & Connectivity
Leverage · Cost & lead time
  • Relocate standard harnesses to Europe / nearshore countries.
  • Use reverse auctions for commoditised connectors and cables.
  • Reduce lead times by sourcing closer to assembly plants.
These three categories generate most of the Asia → Europe dependency reduction by 2026.
Categories already mainly European
For these families, the priority is to keep a strong European footprint and optimise total cost and service rather than changing the sourcing footprint.
  • Plastics & chassis – maintain EU sourcing; develop eco-design and circular materials.
  • Logistics – optimise cost and CO₂ emissions within the existing EU-centric network.
  • Indirect purchases – consolidate suppliers and contracts, focus on TCO and compliance.
