Regulation, Benchmark and Geopolitical Pressure

The external environment alone already pushes Eurimobility toward more resilient, local and compliant supply chains.

EU regulatory pressure, geopolitical instability and industry best practices converge: dependency on Asian suppliers for batteries and electronics is becoming structurally risky and economically fragile.

Regulation
EU rules become hard constraints
Compliance defines sourcing choices.
  • EU Battery Regulation: CO₂ footprint, recycled content, mandatory traceability.
  • EU Taxonomy: green financing depends on responsible supply chains.
  • Duty of Vigilance / ESG laws: mandatory human-rights and environmental risk audits.
  • Non-compliance = fines, blocked products, loss of access to ESG funds.
Geopolitics & Logistics
Asian reliance is structurally exposed
Tensions and logistics shocks increase risk.
  • China–EU tensions, export controls and potential tariffs.
  • Suez crisis, container shortages, shipping volatility.
  • Exposure to sanctions and geopolitical shocks.
  • FX volatility amplifies commodity fluctuations.
Sector Benchmark
Industry leaders move local
Best practices converge toward EU sourcing.
  • Northvolt, ACC, Verkor → EU gigafactories accelerating.
  • OEMs adopt dual sourcing + local-for-local.
  • Long-term strategic partnerships with EU suppliers.
  • Resilience & ESG now included in tenders.
